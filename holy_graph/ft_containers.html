Welcome reader!
I'm sure you've already heard your fair share of rant about this project, so it's finally time for you to start ranting as well.

On a more serious note: As of now this is the biggest solo project in the core, so things might take a little longer than usual and that's okay :)

#Prerequisites

Before starting your work you should know:

  • How to write a templated class and (member) function
  • What the compiler does in the background when compiling your template
    This might seem trivial at first but will come in handy later
  • Why template implementations can't be inside .cpp files
  • The methods and basic internal structure of the containers you have to rewrite
  • How to properly use the std::allocator

One last thing before I let you start:

I'm not putting this in here for no reason.
The subject requires us to write our own tests – I recommend you to do so after every method you complete.
I didn't. And I regretted (and still regret) it.

#During

If your are not sure how to implement something, don't be afraid to open the std-implementation of the containers and look at the relevant snippets. Depending on your platform and toolchain they might be quite hard to read (I'm looking at you LLVM), but it's a good exercise to improve in reading large-scale projects.

Before starting to implement any method, make sure to take a look at:

  • The standard this method appeared in. You will only have to rewrite it if it's C++98
  • Its time complexity, iterator validity and exception safety

Here are some important topics you will learn about during the project:

  • The pros and cons of different containers (data structures)
  • How BSTs and especially self-balancing BSTs work
  • How iterators work and why they are ubiquitous in most languages
  • debugging templates
  • Why iterator_traits exist and how to use them
  • What SFINAE is and how to use enable_if

#Cleaning up

Before sumitting the project make sure:

  • That only the required methods are public in your containers
  • That the container inside your stack has the right name and is protected, not private or public
  • You have all the required operator overloads for your iterators
    • Are you able to construct a const iterator from a normal one?
    • Are you able to compare an iterator with a const iterator and vice versa?
  • You never compare the elements in your BST with anything but the comparison object (yes, this includes not comparing for equality through the == operator)
  • To take a look at the external swap() function. In some cases it's C++11 – in others it's not

  • If a method takes templated parameters, like e.g.

    template<class InputIterator>
vector.assign(InputIterator first, InputIterator last)

    to test this method with the supported iterator categories

#Aftercare

You passed and haven't lost your sanity in the process? Well done!

Here are some questions:

  • Why do some containers store items in random and others in ascending order?
  • Why are there different iterator categories?
  • Do you know the difference between the iterator categories? (especially input_iterator and forward_iterator)
  • What are the pros / cons of BSTs compared to hashmaps? What time and space complexity do both have?

By now you are probably quite fed up with templates and containers, so the exercise is going to be rather simple. I promise :)
